The $75 Flip Flops Jason Kelce Swears By No Matter the Weather

Publish date: 2024-05-08

Travis Kelce isn’t the only person in the Kelce family making headlines. The Philadelphia Eagles player, Jason Kelce, is starting his own trend as he recently stepped out in some nifty flip flops (despite the winter upon us, we should add). The athlete was spotted in an Eagles t-shirt, Cleveland Heights letterman jacket, black joggers, and a stylish Louis Vuitton bag. To finish off the look, Kelce grabbed gold aviator sunglasses and a pair of brown sandals.

OLUKAI Ohana Men's Beach Sandals

OLUKAI Ohana Men's Beach Sandals

The OluKai Ohana Men’s Beach Sandals in dark java/ray that Kelce sported sell for $75 and are advertised to be water resistant, lightweight, and compression molded (and there is a women’s version of the sandals for the same price!). Currently, there are 16 other available colors on Amazon—you can even take advantage of the “try before you buy” option to make sure you achieve that Jason Kelce style before committing to a pair of your own. During a podcast episode of “New Heights,” recorded with his brother, the dad of three explained why he reaches for this kind of footwear (and it’s pretty relatable).

Jason laughs that he wears flip flops all of the time and doesn’t understand why it’s so “newsworthy.” He explains to Travis, “I don’t like bending down to put socks on. It’s easier to just slide them puppies on and head out the door. I am outside for a maximum of—with those sandals—10 seconds.” Jason hilariously commented that he was pictured walking from the hotel to the bus, so he’s very capable of surviving the cold in that short amount of time. The podcaster added, “Do you think I’m trenching through the city of Kansas City in flip flops?! No! If I was doing that, I wouldn’t wear flip flops.”

Apparently, Jason doesn’t pack a pair of shoes, but he sure does always have a good pair of sandals on hand! Now, you too can wear a stunning fit like Jason Kelce—but maybe don’t wear flip flops in the cold for longer than 10 seconds.
